Dataset Name:
geotechnical_time_series_field_cbr_dataset

Problem Statement:
Predict monthly field California Bearing Ratio (CBR) of compacted pavement subgrade sections using repeated geotechnical and environmental observations in a panel time-series dataset. Each row represents one monitored road section at one monthly observation date.

Dataset Structure:
- Rows: 1500
- Time structure: 100 pavement sections observed monthly for 15 months
- Observation period: 2024-01-01 to 2025-03-01
- Sequence key columns: Section_ID and Observation_Date
- Engineering input features: 11
- Regression target: 1

Target Variable Definition:
Field_CBR_Percent
- Definition: In-situ field California Bearing Ratio of the monitored compacted subgrade section at the observation date.
- Unit: %
- Role in ML: Single continuous regression target
- Position in CSV: Last column
- Missing values: None

Sequence Key Columns (not counted among the 11 engineering input features):
- Section_ID
  Description: Unique identifier for each monitored pavement subgrade section.
  Unit: -
  Expected Range: SEC_001 to SEC_100
- Observation_Date
  Description: Monthly observation date for the section.
  Unit: YYYY-MM-DD
  Expected Range: 2024-01-01 to 2025-03-01

Engineering Input Features:
- Gravel_Content_Percent
  Description: Gravel fraction of the section soil by dry mass. Repeated over time for the same section because it is an intrinsic soil property.
  Unit: %
  Expected Range: 0.053 to 44.195
- Sand_Content_Percent
  Description: Sand fraction of the section soil by dry mass. Together with gravel and fines, it represents soil gradation.
  Unit: %
  Expected Range: 24.947 to 78.988
- Fines_Content_Percent
  Description: Percent passing the No. 200 sieve, including silt and clay-sized particles.
  Unit: %
  Expected Range: 9.621 to 73.186
- Plasticity_Index_Percent
  Description: Plasticity index used as an indicator of fines plasticity and moisture sensitivity.
  Unit: %
  Expected Range: 2.000 to 35.089
- Optimum_Moisture_Content_Percent
  Description: Optimum moisture content from compaction testing for the section soil.
  Unit: %
  Expected Range: 6.328 to 21.500
- Maximum_Dry_Density_kN_per_m3
  Description: Maximum dry density from the laboratory compaction curve for the section soil.
  Unit: kN/m^3
  Expected Range: 16.344 to 21.400
- Natural_Moisture_Content_Percent
  Description: Measured field moisture content at the monthly observation.
  Unit: %
  Expected Range: 4.441 to 26.236
- Degree_of_Compaction_Percent
  Description: Achieved field dry density expressed as a percentage of the section maximum dry density.
  Unit: %
  Expected Range: 94.707 to 101.262
- Rainfall_Last_7_Days_mm
  Description: Total rainfall during the 7 days before the observation date, representing short-term wetting.
  Unit: mm
  Expected Range: 0.000 to 140.000
- Rainfall_Last_30_Days_mm
  Description: Total rainfall during the 30 days before the observation date, representing recent seasonal wetness.
  Unit: mm
  Expected Range: 5.000 to 300.000
- Groundwater_Depth_m
  Description: Depth from ground surface to the groundwater table near the section at the observation date.
  Unit: m
  Expected Range: 0.350 to 4.569

How the Synthetic Data Was Constructed:
1. The dataset was built as a panel time series rather than a single-site series. One hundred subgrade sections were generated, each with fixed intrinsic soil properties.
2. Each section was assigned to a realistic latent soil family: granular soils with fines, silty soils, lean clays, or fat clays.
3. Static section properties were generated first:
   - gravel, sand, and fines contents
   - plasticity index
   - optimum moisture content
   - maximum dry density
4. Monthly environmental forcing was then generated using section-specific rainfall seasonality and variability. Rainfall in the last 7 days and last 30 days was simulated with wet and dry periods plus occasional storm pulses.
5. Monthly groundwater depth was generated as a seasonal variable influenced by rainfall and section-specific baseline drainage conditions.
6. Natural moisture content was generated as a time-evolving state influenced by:
   - recent rainfall
   - groundwater depth
   - section plasticity
   - previous moisture state
7. Degree of compaction was generated as a time-evolving field condition that tends to reduce slightly under wetter conditions while remaining within a realistic field compaction range.
8. Field_CBR_Percent was generated from a nonlinear strength model using:
   - intrinsic soil quality from gradation, plasticity, and compaction properties
   - current natural moisture relative to optimum moisture
   - degree of compaction
   - recent rainfall
   - groundwater depth
   - moderate hidden temporal persistence
9. Realistic noise was added so relationships are useful for machine learning without becoming unrealistically perfect.

Engineering Logic Behind the Relationships:
- Higher gravel and sand contents generally increase field CBR because coarser soils resist penetration better than highly plastic fine-grained soils.
- Higher fines content and higher plasticity index generally reduce field CBR because these soils are more moisture-sensitive and weaker when wet.
- Higher maximum dry density and higher degree of compaction generally increase field CBR because denser packing improves resistance to penetration.
- Natural moisture content near or below optimum generally supports stronger behavior than overly wet conditions. Excess moisture causes notable CBR reduction.
- Higher recent rainfall and shallower groundwater generally increase subgrade wetness and therefore reduce field CBR.
- Because the dataset is time-series, monthly observations exhibit continuity rather than independent random jumps. Moisture and strength states evolve over time.

Missing Data Strategy:
- Missing values were introduced only in the 11 engineering input feature columns.
- Missingness was applied independently and randomly by column.
- Missing values are represented as NaN in the CSV.
- The exact missing percentages are:
- Gravel_Content_Percent: 8.1% missing
- Sand_Content_Percent: 8.0% missing
- Fines_Content_Percent: 5.7% missing
- Plasticity_Index_Percent: 5.4% missing
- Optimum_Moisture_Content_Percent: 9.5% missing
- Maximum_Dry_Density_kN_per_m3: 9.9% missing
- Natural_Moisture_Content_Percent: 7.4% missing
- Degree_of_Compaction_Percent: 7.9% missing
- Rainfall_Last_7_Days_mm: 9.3% missing
- Rainfall_Last_30_Days_mm: 5.9% missing
- Groundwater_Depth_m: 6.2% missing
- Section_ID and Observation_Date were kept complete so the time ordering and section grouping remain intact.
- The target column contains no missing values.

Intended Educational Use:
- Beginner machine learning for civil and geotechnical engineering students
- Time-series and panel-data preprocessing
- Missing data imputation
- Feature engineering from timestamps
- Grouped train/test splitting by time or by section
- Exploratory data analysis and visualization
- Regression modeling using linear models, random forests, gradient boosting, or XGBoost-like methods
- Optional advanced exercises using lag features and rolling statistics created by students

Assumptions:
- The dataset represents compacted pavement subgrade sections rather than base course aggregates or organic soils.
- The field CBR target is intended to mimic repeated field strength measurements affected by seasonal moisture changes.
- The soil index properties are assumed constant for each section over the observation period, while moisture, rainfall, groundwater depth, compaction state, and strength evolve over time.
- The dataset is synthetic and educational. It should not be used directly for engineering design without site-specific sampling and testing.
